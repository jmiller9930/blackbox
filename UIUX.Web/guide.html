How BLACK BOX works

This guide explains the system in plain language so an operator, internal teammate, or consumer can understand how the portal, Anna, the engine, Billy, and PiBot fit together.

Overview

BLACK BOX is one system with clearly separated parts. The portal is the front door. The engine core is the source of truth and the place where governed status, decisions, and controls live. Anna is the strategist and analyst. Billy is the execution bot / market connector. PiBot is the edge device that stays under user control and holds local secret information.

The portal does not replace the engine. It gives humans a structured way to see what the system is doing, interact with Anna, and manage supported workflows such as account access, status visibility, and eventually PiBot connection and recovery.

How the pieces fit together

  • Portal UI: the human-facing shell and workflow surface.
  • Engine core: the source of truth, API, status, controls, and artifacts.
  • Anna: strategist / analyst intelligence surfaced through the engine.
  • Billy: execution bot / market connector surfaced through the engine.
  • PiBot: user-owned edge device that keeps local secrets and executes on the edge.
  • API boundary: the approved contract between the portal and the engine.

In short: BLACK BOX is the platform, the portal is how people use it, the engine is the operational brain, and PiBot is the edge extension that remains under local user control.

How to work with Anna

Anna is conversation-first. Humans should talk to Anna in normal language by default. Command tags exist only where deterministic routing, logging, or governed handling is required.

  • Ask for explanations in plain language.
  • Challenge Anna if the thesis or logic looks weak.
  • Ask for status or review when you want current operating state.
  • Use explicit training staging language when you want something to become training.

Anna should not silently self-update from casual conversation. Training suggestions stay conversational until they are explicitly staged.

Plain-language intents Anna should recognize

explanation request, challenge, counter-argument, training suggestion, status request, and review request

Curriculum and training

Conversation and training are not the same thing. If you give Anna a suggestion that looks training-relevant, she should classify it first, explain why, and ask whether you want it staged.

Anna's compact training packet

  • classification
  • why
  • recommended_next_action
  • confirm?

Human confirmation grammar

  • stage it
  • revise it
  • leave it

In `v1`, if you say revise it, Anna should return one revised candidate only. Training becomes meaningful when it moves through governed review, simulation, and where allowed later, gated live evidence.

Paper trades and real trades

A paper trade is treated as a real decision event without real capital movement. This lets Anna test trade logic, collect outcome data, and learn from the result without risking funds.

  • Simulation / backtest: replay or stored-data testing.
  • Paper trade: live-data path, fake/non-custodial execution.
  • Real trade: gated live execution with stricter approval and control requirements.

The expected direction is that Anna proves herself in the paper-trade lane before any actual-trade authorization is considered. Passing paper performance should be a promotion gate, not an implied right to trade live.

PiBot connection and recovery

PiBot is the edge device. It keeps the secret information locally. BLACK BOX should not hold exchange secrets, seed phrases, or private keys. The portal should therefore own the user-facing pairing and recovery flow, while PiBot proves its identity from the edge.

  • Device still intact: recover / re-pair through the portal with device proof.
  • Device wiped or reimaged: treat it as a new device and start over.
  • Secret handling: secret information is re-entered on PiBot, not stored in BLACK BOX.

What the main portal statuses mean

  • Anna status: what Anna is doing, thinking about, or waiting on.
  • Billy / exchange status: whether the execution path appears online and trade-capable.
  • Winning / losing: current performance state, not a guarantee of future results.
  • Training / learning: what is under review, staged, or being validated.
  • Edge-bot status: whether PiBot or other edge devices appear connected.

The portal should show readable summaries backed by structured engine artifacts rather than forcing users to reconstruct system truth from raw logs.
